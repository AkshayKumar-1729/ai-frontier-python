What U2-Net learned
Trained on DUTS — 10,553 photos manually labeled pixel-by-pixel. For every pixel, it predicts: "how likely is this to be the main subject?" It doesn't "know" what a background is — it learned what humans consider worth keeping.
⚡ Canva's "Remove Background", Adobe Photoshop's magic wand, Zoom's virtual background — all use the same U-Net idea. rembg is the open-source version. 3 lines.

How it works
DistilBERT was trained on the Stanford Sentiment Treebank — movie reviews labeled positive/negative. It learned word associations and context: "not bad" → positive. It understands that "not" flips the meaning.

How DeepFace knows
Trained on FER2013 — 35,887 facial images, labeled by humans. It maps your face to emotion patterns. Under the hood: a mini-Xception CNN classifies expression, a VGG-Face variant estimates age.
⚡ Hirevue used emotion AI to filter job candidates from interview videos. It was discontinued — the training data skewed toward Western expressions, introducing bias against other cultures.

What Gradio actually is
FastAPI (Python web server) + auto-generated HTML/JS frontend + a tunnel to gradio.live. The "backend" is your function. The "frontend" — what appears on your phone — is generated automatically. This is real web development, with training wheels.
⚡ ngrok, Cloudflare Tunnel, GitHub Codespaces — all the same tunnel concept. Day 10 we use raw FastAPI. Today, Gradio gets you there in 4 lines.
